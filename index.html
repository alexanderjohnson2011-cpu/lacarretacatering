The Chef

Meet Denise

Denise grew up inside a kitchen. Her family's restaurant, La Carreta, has been feeding Thousand Oaks with Salvadoran heart for nearly two decades — and she absorbed everything: the patience, the intention, the belief that food is an act of love.

But Denise's cooking never stayed in one lane. She's personally vegan, and that shapes how she approaches every dish — plant-based or not. She brings a chef's technique to Latin-inspired flavors, puts her own spin on Italian-American classics, and creates plant-forward menus that genuinely surprise people.

The thread running through all of it: every dish she makes feels like someone took real care with it. Because she did.

What She Cooks

Not one cuisine.
One philosophy.

The through-line isn't a flag on a map. It's technique, care, and the belief that every dish deserves to be the best version of itself.

🌮

Latin-Inspired

Bold spices, bright acids, achiote and citrus and chiles. Dishes with depth and warmth that make you feel the sun even indoors. Rooted in Central American tradition, expanded by curiosity.

Italian-American

Slow braises, hand-made pasta, Sunday-style sauces that cook all day. Her Italian-American dishes are cooked from memory — the kind that smells like someone loves you.
